Research profile

I am an M.S. & Ph.D. integrated student in Electrical Engineering & Computer Sciences at DGIST, working with Prof. Donghoon Shin on industrial AI security and critical infrastructure resilience.

Yooshin Kim

My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, cybersecurity, and cyber-physical systems. I focus on how industrial control systems can become more secure and explainable through anomaly detection, passive authentication, privacy-preserving analytics, and contextual digital twins.

The through-line is pragmatic security: methods should work with real operational constraints, reduce analyst burden, and help teams understand what is happening inside critical infrastructure systems.

Focus areas

Problems I care about

Anomaly detection for ICS

Building models that identify abnormal behaviors in industrial environments and remain useful under drift, operational noise, and limited supervision.

Passive security mechanisms

Studying low-friction security signals, especially keystroke-driven authentication, that can improve protection without introducing operator fatigue.

Context-aware system understanding

Combining system context, signal relationships, and digital twin concepts to make industrial monitoring more interpretable and actionable.
